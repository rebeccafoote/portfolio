Rebecca Foote is a South Korean-born, Italian-raised, Jersey girl living in Brooklyn. She recently finished developing her senior fashion design thesis at Parsons School of Design with a focus in materiality.

Much of her work is inspired by origami and paper folding as a means to create self-standing structures. Coupled with that, Rebecca has often used denim to serve as a base for textile development and exploration. She has a deep love for the physical art of making (and does not shy away when numbers get involved). Relishing in the tangibility of construction, she explores handcrafted techniques driven by materials that lend purpose and quality.

Design for Rebecca stems from a very personal and singular level despite the outcome often being a very public expression, and she continually explores that balance.
